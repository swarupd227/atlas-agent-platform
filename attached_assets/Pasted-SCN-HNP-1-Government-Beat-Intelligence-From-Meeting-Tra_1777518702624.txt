SCN-HNP-1 Government Beat Intelligence — From Meeting Transcript to Published Story

Sub-segment: Metro & Regional Newsrooms — Government Accountability | Newsrooms: Houston Chronicle, San Antonio Express-News, Austin American-Statesman

Business Problem
Hearst’s Assembly tool already does what no other newspaper group has done at scale: it automatically transcribes government meetings, keyword-detects topics of interest, and makes the transcripts searchable for reporters. Between May 2024 and April 2025, Assembly transcribed 13,119 hours of government meetings — more than 547 days’ worth of content. But the bottleneck has simply moved: reporters now have the transcripts but still face 2–4 hours of manual reading, note-taking, and story-building per meeting to determine whether a publishable story exists.

In the context of Hurricane Mara, the immediate challenge is acute: 47 Houston City Council and Harris County emergency preparedness meetings from the past 90 days are sitting in the Assembly corpus. Hidden in those transcripts is the critical context reporters need right now — past flood mitigation promises, infrastructure commitments, contractor names, elected official statements, prior emergency response critiques. Without Atlas, a reporter would need 3–5 days to synthesise all 47 transcripts. Atlas does it in 45 minutes.

⚙ Platform Intelligence Setup in Atlas
Outcome Contract
Contract Name: HNP Government Beat Intelligence — Assembly-to-Investigation Pipeline
Risk Tier: High — outputs inform published journalism with legal and reputational stakes
KPIs: Time from Meeting Transcription to Reporter Brief (<45 min) | Story Angles per Brief (2–4 ranked) | Evidence Confidence Mean (>0.82) | Reporter Approval Rate (>75% of surfaced angles) | FOIA Conversion Rate (filed within 24 hrs of angle approval)
Drift Threshold: Reporter rejection rate above 30% for two consecutive weeks triggers KB review and extraction model recalibration

Knowledge Bases (KB System)
•  HNP Assembly Corpus — All government meeting transcripts in the Assembly system — Houston: 47 transcripts (past 90 days), San Antonio: 31 transcripts, Austin: 28 transcripts. Indexed with meeting type, date, attending officials, keyword tags.
•  HNP Investigative Journalism Standards — Internal HNP editorial standards for evidence requirements, attribution rules, and what constitutes a publishable fact vs. an unverified claim. Agent outputs are checked against these standards before surfacing to reporters.
•  Texas Government Entity Ontology — Structured knowledge of Texas government structure: Harris County, City of Houston, HCFCD, TCEQ, TxDOT — with official names, jurisdiction boundaries, FOIA officer contacts, and public records laws.
•  Elected Official Profile Index — Named entities for all covered elected officials with role, term dates, voting history on major local ordinances, and known campaign contributors.

MCP Server Integrations
•  HNP Assembly MCP — get_transcripts, search_transcript_corpus, get_transcript_by_meeting, get_keyword_alerts — full Assembly corpus access
•  HNP Knowledge Base MCP — get_investigative_standards, get_entity_ontology, get_official_profile, get_jurisdiction_foia_rules
•  HNP Public Records MCP — submit_foia_request, get_foia_status, search_prior_requests, get_agency_officer — interfaces with government FOIA portals
•  HNP CMS MCP — create_story_draft, assign_to_reporter, set_story_tags, set_seo_fields — pushes story skeletons into Hearst’s CMS

Governance Policies
Human Reporter Gate — no story draft enters the CMS without explicit reporter angle approval; the AI never initiates a publication pathway
Source Attribution Requirement — every fact in the story skeleton must carry a source citation to a specific transcript, timestamp, and speaker; uncited claims are blocked
Publication Boundary — all agent outputs are marked DRAFT — NOT FOR PUBLICATION; removal of this mark requires editorial sign-off through normal CMS workflow
FOIA Accuracy Gate — FOIA request letters require reporter acknowledgement before filing; the agent cannot file unilaterally
