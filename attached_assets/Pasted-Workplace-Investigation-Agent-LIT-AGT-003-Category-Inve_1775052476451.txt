Workplace Investigation Agent
LIT-AGT-003  |  Category: Investigations
4.1 Description
Supports the full lifecycle of workplace investigations into discrimination, harassment, retaliation, whistleblower complaints, and policy violations. Maps to Littler's Investigations and Discrimination & Harassment practice areas. The agent structures investigation plans, manages witness interview preparation, tracks evidence collection, analyzes findings against legal standards, and drafts investigation reports. It ensures investigations are thorough, timely, well-documented, and legally defensible.
4.2 Workflow Steps
23.	1. Receive complaint (formal charge, hotline report, manager escalation, litigation hold)
24.	2. Classify complaint: discrimination, harassment, retaliation, theft, policy violation, safety
25.	3. Assess scope, severity, and urgency; recommend investigator and investigation level
26.	4. Generate investigation plan: witnesses to interview, documents to collect, timeline
27.	5. Prepare witness interview outlines with legally required admonitions and key questions
28.	6. Track evidence collection: documents, emails, video, physical evidence, electronic records
29.	7. Analyze witness statements for consistency, corroboration, and credibility indicators
30.	8. Apply relevant legal standards (Title VII, ADA, ADEA, state equivalents) to factual findings
31.	9. Draft investigation summary report with findings, credibility assessments, and conclusions
32.	10. Recommend corrective action based on severity, precedent, and proportionality
33.	11. Generate litigation hold notice if necessary and archive complete investigation file

4.3 Common Ontology
The following entities and attributes form the shared data model referenced by this agent:
Entity	Key Attributes
Complaint	complaint ID, type, complainant, respondent, date, severity, status
Investigation	investigation ID, scope, investigator, timeline, status, privilege designation
Witness	name, role, relationship to parties, availability, interview status, credibility notes
Evidence	type (document, email, video, testimony), source, collection date, chain of custody
Finding	factual determination, legal standard applied, supporting evidence, confidence level
Corrective Action	type (warning, suspension, termination, training, policy change), proportionality

4.5 Eval Dataset
Recommended evaluation data and benchmarks for validating agent accuracy and performance:
•	500+ historical investigation files with known outcomes and corrective actions
•	Complaint classification accuracy: type and severity prediction against experienced investigator assessment
•	Investigation plan completeness: AI-generated plans vs. attorney-created plans
•	Legal standard application: correct identification of applicable laws and burden-shifting frameworks
•	Report quality: attorney review scores on thoroughness, objectivity, and legal defensibility
•	Timeline adherence: investigation completion within policy-mandated timeframes

4.6 Production Runbooks
Operational procedures for handling common issues and failure scenarios:
Scenario	Procedure
Imminent Threat Detection	Emergency escalation to law enforcement, security, and senior management
Conflict of Interest Discovery	Investigator reassignment procedure, scope adjustment
Privilege Waiver Risk	Identification of inadvertent disclosure risks, remediation steps
Complainant Retaliation Alert	Monitoring protocol, protective measures, documentation
Parallel Government Investigation	Coordination procedure, document hold, outside counsel notification
Investigation Integrity Breach	Evidence tampering detection, chain of custody documentation, escalation

4.7 Regulatory Compliance & Policies
Applicable regulations and compliance requirements:
•	Title VII of the Civil Rights Act: Prompt and thorough investigation obligation (Faragher/Ellerth defense)
•	ADA: Reasonable accommodation interactive process during investigations
•	ADEA: Age discrimination investigation requirements and burden-shifting frameworks
•	State Fair Employment Laws: California FEHA, New York Human Rights Law, Illinois HRA, etc.
•	Whistleblower Protection: SOX, Dodd-Frank, and state whistleblower statutes
•	Attorney-Client Privilege / Work Product: Upjohn warnings, privilege maintenance during investigations
