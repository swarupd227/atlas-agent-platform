Litigation Case Management Agent
LIT-AGT-004  |  Category: Litigation
5.1 Description
Manages single-plaintiff employment litigation from charge intake through resolution. Maps to Littler's CaseSmart platform and Litigation & Trials practice area. The agent tracks deadlines, manages document production, analyzes case strengths and weaknesses, monitors budgets, and provides data-driven insights for case strategy. Designed to handle high volumes of EEOC charges and single-plaintiff lawsuits with consistent quality and cost efficiency.
5.2 Workflow Steps
34.	1. Receive new matter (EEOC charge, state agency complaint, or lawsuit filing)
35.	2. Conflict check and matter opening with billing codes and staffing assignment
36.	3. Analyze charge/complaint: extract claims, parties, factual allegations, relief sought
37.	4. Calculate key deadlines: response due dates, statute of limitations, discovery cutoffs
38.	5. Assess case strength: evaluate claims, defenses, and early resolution opportunities
39.	6. Generate initial case assessment memo with strategy recommendations
40.	7. Manage discovery: track production obligations, review schedules, privilege logging
41.	8. Monitor litigation budget against alternative fee or case budget
42.	9. Prepare dispositive motion analysis: identify viable summary judgment grounds
43.	10. Track settlement negotiations: demand/offer history, authority levels, comparables
44.	11. Update matter analytics dashboard: case trends, outcomes, cost metrics

5.3 Common Ontology
The following entities and attributes form the shared data model referenced by this agent:
Entity	Key Attributes
Matter	matter ID, type (EEOC charge, lawsuit), status, assigned team, billing arrangement
Claim	claim type (discrimination, harassment, retaliation, wrongful termination), statute, elements

Discovery	type (interrogatories, document requests, depositions), status, cost, privilege issues
Assessment	strength rating, risk level, recommended strategy, settlement range, confidence
Budget	arrangement type (hourly, AFA, blended), projected spend, actual spend, variance

5.5 Eval Dataset
Recommended evaluation data and benchmarks for validating agent accuracy and performance:
•	10,000+ historical EEOC charges and lawsuits with known outcomes (settlement, judgment, dismissal)
•	Deadline calculation accuracy: computed dates vs. actual court-imposed deadlines
•	Case assessment reliability: early strength ratings correlated with actual outcomes
•	Budget forecast accuracy: projected vs. actual spend by phase and matter type
•	Settlement valuation: comparable-based ranges vs. actual resolution amounts
•	CaseSmart benchmark data: cost per charge, average cycle time, outcome distribution
