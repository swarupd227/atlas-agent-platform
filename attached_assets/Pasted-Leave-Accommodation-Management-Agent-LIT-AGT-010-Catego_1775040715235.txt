Leave & Accommodation Management Agent
LIT-AGT-010  |  Category: Leave & Accommodation
11.1 Description
Navigates the complex intersection of federal, state, and local leave laws and disability accommodation obligations. Maps to Littler's Leave and Accommodation practice area. The agent determines leave eligibility, manages the interactive accommodation process, tracks concurrent leave running, and ensures compliance with FMLA, ADA, state paid family leave, and local sick leave ordinances - one of the most complex compliance areas for multi-state employers.
11.2 Workflow Steps
100.	1. Receive leave or accommodation request (employee notice, certification, medical documentation)
101.	2. Determine all applicable leave laws based on employee location, employer size, and reason
102.	3. Calculate eligibility under each applicable law (FMLA, state FMLA, paid leave, sick leave)
103.	4. Identify concurrent leave running and stacking implications
104.	5. For accommodations: initiate interactive process, identify potential accommodations, assess undue hardship
105.	6. Track leave usage against entitlements with real-time balance calculations
106.	7. Manage medical certification requirements and recertification timelines
107.	8. Generate required notices: FMLA designation, state-specific notices, ADA correspondence
108.	9. Assess fitness-for-duty certification requirements at return from leave
109.	10. Identify leave abuse indicators while respecting protected leave rights
110.	11. Track leave trends and accommodation patterns for proactive compliance

11.7 Regulatory Compliance & Policies
Applicable regulations and compliance requirements:
•	FMLA (Family and Medical Leave Act): 12/26 weeks unpaid leave, eligibility, certification, reinstatement
•	ADA (Americans with Disabilities Act): Reasonable accommodation, interactive process, undue hardship
•	Pregnancy Workers Fairness Act (PWFA): Pregnancy-related accommodations
•	State Paid Family Leave: California PFL, New York PFL, Washington PFML, and 10+ other state programs
•	State/Local Sick Leave: Mandatory paid sick leave in 15+ states and numerous municipalities
•	USERRA: Military leave and reemployment rights
