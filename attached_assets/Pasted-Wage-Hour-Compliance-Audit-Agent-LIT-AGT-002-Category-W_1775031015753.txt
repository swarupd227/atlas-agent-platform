Wage & Hour Compliance Audit Agent
LIT-AGT-002  |  Category: Wage & Hour
3.1 Description
Conducts comprehensive wage and hour compliance audits across FLSA and state wage laws. Analyzes employee classifications (exempt vs. non-exempt), overtime calculations, meal and rest break compliance, minimum wage adherence, pay stub requirements, and predictive scheduling obligations. Maps to Littler's Wage & Hour practice area and Audit Quarterback (Audit QB) product, providing data-driven risk assessments that help employers proactively identify and remediate compliance issues before they become litigation.
3.2 Workflow Steps
12.	1. Ingest client payroll data, time records, job descriptions, and compensation structures
13.	2. Map employee classifications against FLSA exemption tests (executive, administrative, professional, computer, outside sales)
14.	3. Analyze state-specific exemption criteria for all applicable jurisdictions
15.	4. Review overtime calculations against federal and state overtime rules (daily, weekly, 7th day, etc.)
16.	5. Audit meal and rest break compliance (California, Oregon, Washington, and other state-specific rules)
17.	6. Verify minimum wage compliance including tip credits, sub-minimum, and local ordinances
18.	7. Check pay stub and record-keeping compliance by jurisdiction
19.	8. Evaluate predictive/fair scheduling compliance for applicable jurisdictions and industries
20.	9. Assess piece-rate, commission, and bonus inclusion in regular rate calculations
21.	10. Generate risk-scored findings report with remediation recommendations and exposure estimates
22.	11. Track remediation progress and schedule follow-up audits

3.3 Common Ontology
The following entities and attributes form the shared data model referenced by this agent:
Entity	Key Attributes
Employee	employee ID, job title, classification (exempt/non-exempt), duties, salary, location
Exemption Test	test type (duties, salary basis, salary level), jurisdiction, threshold, result
Payroll Record	pay period, hours worked, regular rate, overtime hours, deductions, net pay
Violation	type, severity, affected employees count, back-pay exposure, penalty exposure
Jurisdiction Rule	state/city, topic (overtime, meal break, minimum wage), rule details, effective date
Audit Finding	finding ID, risk level (high/medium/low), description, remediation, exposure estimate

3.4 Claude Agent Skills
The following skills should be developed and registered as context for this agent:

Classification Analysis Skill	Evaluates job duties against FLSA and state exemption tests with confidence scoring
Overtime Calculation Engine Skill	Computes correct overtime under all applicable methods (weekly, daily, 7th day, ABC test)

3.5 Eval Dataset
Recommended evaluation data and benchmarks for validating agent accuracy and performance:
•	5,000+ employee classification scenarios with known correct exempt/non-exempt determinations
•	Overtime calculation test cases across all state methodologies (CA daily OT, OR daily OT, standard weekly, etc.)
•	Meal and rest break violation detection against manually reviewed time punch data
•	Minimum wage compliance across 200+ jurisdictions with known rates and effective dates
•	Exposure calculation accuracy: model outputs vs. actual settlement/judgment amounts
•	Classification edge cases: hybrid roles, working managers, commissioned employees, gig workers

3.7 Regulatory Compliance & Policies
Applicable regulations and compliance requirements:
•	Fair Labor Standards Act (FLSA): Federal minimum wage, overtime, record-keeping, child labor
•	State Wage & Hour Laws: California Labor Code, New York Labor Law, and 48 other state-specific regimes
•	Local Ordinances: Municipal minimum wage, paid sick leave, fair scheduling (SF, Seattle, NYC, Chicago, etc.)
•	Department of Labor (DOL) Investigations: Cooperation obligations, document production requirements
•	PAGA (Private Attorneys General Act): California-specific representative action compliance
•	State-Specific Pay Transparency: Colorado, California, New York, Washington pay range disclosure requirements
