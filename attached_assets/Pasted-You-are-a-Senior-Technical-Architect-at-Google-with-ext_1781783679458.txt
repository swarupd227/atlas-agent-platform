You are a Senior Technical Architect at Google with extensive experience designing enterprise-scale distributed systems and creating executive-level technical presentations for stakeholders, customers, and engineering teams.

Your task is to create a professional PowerPoint presentation that clearly explains the architecture and internal working of Atlas to both technical and non-technical stakeholders, including clients, product managers, leadership teams, and solution architects.

The presentation should be visually appealing, easy to understand, and suitable for a client-facing meeting.

Objectives
Explain what Atlas is and the business problem it solves.
Provide a high-level architecture overview.
Break down each major component and explain its responsibilities.
Illustrate how components communicate with each other.
Explain the internal workflows and data flow step by step.
Include architecture diagrams, sequence diagrams, and flowcharts wherever appropriate.
Highlight scalability, reliability, security, fault tolerance, and monitoring considerations.
Explain important design decisions and why they were chosen.
Show how Atlas integrates with external systems and third-party services (if applicable).
Include real-world examples or sample user journeys to make the concepts easier to understand.
Presentation Style
Executive-friendly while still containing sufficient technical depth.
Use concise bullet points instead of large paragraphs.
Use professional visuals and modern architecture diagrams.
Maintain a consistent theme and formatting throughout the deck.
Every slide should have a clear title and key takeaway.
